note

You learn fast when you have no choice.

Moving to Mexico

I moved from Turkey to Mexico as a teenager. Spanish was new. The school was new. The food was new. I spent the first few months mostly quiet because I didn't have the words yet. You pick up a language fast when you need it to make a friend.

Getting out of my head

I used to not talk in groups. Even in Turkish. The new country made it worse for a while, then it forced me out of it. If you're the new kid and you don't speak, you stay invisible. So I started speaking. Badly at first. Then less badly.

Being the translator at home

My family doesn't speak much Spanish. So I'm the one who calls the bank, the doctor, the landlord. By 15 I had handled more grown-up phone calls than most people my age. It teaches you how the world actually works behind the screen.

Doing it on my own

I manage my own schoolwork, my own projects, my own job. Nobody reminds me to write my IB internal assessments or my college essays. If I don't do it, it doesn't get done. I figured that out early.

What I want next

A Computer Science degree in Europe on a full scholarship. A product that more than a thousand people actually use. Eventually, something of my own.
